Lemon Tart

description

This lemon tart recipe uses the sabayon and bain-marie methods to create a velvety lemon custard. The crust is made of pine nuts, giving this lemon tart the perfect balance of nutty, sweet, and tart. The flavor is similar to lemon bars, but it's elegant and fancy enough to serve up at dinner parties served with some whipped cream and fresh berries. Check out the step by step recipe below including helpful photos and all the tips you need to make the best lemon tart.

    The Sabayon

  1. 1. Bring about 1 1/2 inches of water to a boil in a pot that is slightly smaller than the diameter of the bowl you will be using for the sabayon. Meanwhile, in a large metal bowl, whisk the eggs, yolks, and sugar for about 1 minute, or until the mixture is smooth.

  2. 2. Set the bowl over the pot and, using a large whisk, whip the mixture while you turn the bowl (for even heating). After about 2 minutes, when the eggs are foamy and have thickened, add one-third of the lemon juice. Continue to whisk vigorously and, when the mixture thickens again, add another one-third of the lemon juice. Whisk until the mixture thickens again, then add the remaining lemon juice.

  4. 3. Continue whisking vigorously, still turning the bowl, until the mixture is thickened and light in color and sticks to a spoon or whisk. The total cooking time should be 8 to 10 minutes.

  5. 4.Turn off the heat and leave the bowl over the water. Whisk in the butter a piece at a time. The sabayon may loosen slightly, but it will thicken and set as it cools.

  6. 5.Pour the warm sabayon into the tart crust and place the pan on a baking sheet.

  8. 6. Preheat the broiler. While the sabayon is still warm, place the tart under the broiler. Leaving the door open, brown the top of the sabayon, rotating the tart if necessary for even color; this will take only a few seconds, so do not leave the oven. Remove the tart from the broiler and let it sit for at least 1 hour before serving. Serve at room temperature or cold.
